## Competitive Landscape

### RE/MAX
- **Strengths:** Technology-forward (most agents have custom websites), high agent mobility (agents can switch brokerages easily), strong brand recognition
- **Weaknesses:** Fragmented (independent agents act alone), less institutional support, fewer luxury properties
- **Our edge:** National coordination, institutional backing, Homie AI advantage

### Century 21
- **Strengths:** Strong in mid-market homes, good digital presence, regional strength
- **Weaknesses:** Declining market share, less luxury presence, weaker institutional support
- **Our edge:** Scale, legacy brand, network effects

### Coldwell Banker
- **Strengths:** Luxury property dominance, international network, high-value transactions
- **Weaknesses:** Premium positioning limits buyer base, fewer agents in smaller cities
- **Our edge:** Royal LePage Signature competes in luxury; broader geographic coverage

### Local Independents
- **Strengths:** Community knowledge, personalized service, lower overhead
- **Weaknesses:** No MLS access (in most provinces), no national reach, limited marketing budget
- **Our edge:** MLS access (through CREA), national referral network, institutional marketing resources

## Referral Network Protocol

### Inter-Brokerage Referrals (Listing to Buying)
When a Royal LePage agent lists a home and the buyer is represented by a different Royal LePage agent:
- **Referral split:** 50/50 split on buyer commission (unless agent negotiates otherwise)
- **Process:** Listing agent facilitates intro; both agents close deal
- **Network effect:** Royal LePage's 18K agents = more referrals within network vs. competitors with fewer agents

### Out-of-Province Referrals
Buyer relocating from Toronto to Vancouver → Homie tags `needs-referral: Vancouver`. Agent in Toronto makes handoff call to Vancouver agent, introduces buyer.

**Value:** Buyer gets white-glove handoff (not Zillow shuffle); Vancouver agent gets warm lead; Toronto agent gets referral fee (~25% of buyer commission).

### Agent-to-Agent Referrals (Same Brokerage)
Agent specializes in condos, buyer wants detached → Referral to detached-home specialist. Both agents cooperate; split commissions per company policy.

## Homage (Offer & CMA Platform)

### What Homage Does
- **Comparative Market Analysis (CMA):** Agent prepares in 15 minutes vs. 1–2 hours manually. Includes recent comps, days-on-market, price trends.
- **Offer Writing & Negotiation:** Agent inputs offer details; Homage formats legally compliant offer within REALTOR/provincial rules. Agent still negotiates; Homage just handles paperwork.
- **Listing Presentations:** Agent uses templates to show sellers comparable sales, pricing strategy, marketing plan (all in Homage).

### Competitive Win
RE/MAX agents do CMAs in 1.5 hours; Royal LePage agents with Homage do it in 15 minutes. Faster agent availability = faster lead conversions.

### Homie Connection
Homie qualifies lead → passes LPMAMA to agent → agent uses Homage to prepare CMA → lead gets detailed analysis in 24 hours. Competitor sends generic email saying "I'll get back to you."

## Competitive Threats & Responses

### Threat: "Zillow / Realtor.com has more listings"
**Reality:** They don't — they syndicate from MLS. Royal LePage agents have same access.
**Response:** "Our agents can set up custom searches that feed your inbox every day. You won't miss a property. Plus, you get our agent's insight on neighborhood trends, not just data."

### Threat: Buyer Prefers Discount Brokerage (1–2% Commission)
**Reality:** Discount brokerages offer lower seller commission; buyer commission is unaffected.
**Response:** "We compete on value, not just commission. Our agents close faster (better negotiating), provide market insights (CMAs, pricing), and have a national network if you relocate."

### Threat: Agent Says "I'll Use My Own AI/CRM"
**Reality:** Individual agents using ChatGPT don't have institutional lead intake; leads still go through manual process.
**Response:** Homie is brokerage-level infrastructure; all leads flow through GHL automatically, freeing up agent time for closing.

### Threat: International Buyer Prefers U.S. Brokerage Experience
**Reality:** Canada has different rules (CMHC, land transfer tax, provinces).
**Response:** Homie is Canada-native. We explain FHSA, provincial differences, financing. U.S. brokerages don't.

## Seasonal / Promotional Opportunities

### Spring (March–May) — "Market Heating Up"
- Messaging: "Spring is prime time — inventory is rising, but so is competition. Get pre-approved and ready."

- Messaging: "Summer is less busy. Sellers tend to be more motivated. Room to negotiate."

- Messaging: "Families want to move before school starts. This is peak volume."

### Winter (December–February) — "Motivated Sellers"
- Messaging: "Winter sees fewer buyers and motivated sellers. Great for negotiation."
- Agent focus: Creative financing, inspections, leverage negotiations
