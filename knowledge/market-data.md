## Days on Market & Inventory

### High-Competition Markets
- **Toronto, Vancouver**: 20–30 days average time on market
- **Active listings**: Below 2 months of inventory (buyer's market shifts occur at 6+ months)
- **Price negotiations**: Sellers have advantage; bidding wars possible on desirable properties

### Balanced Markets
- **Ottawa, Montreal**: 35–50 days average
- **Active listings**: 3–4 months of inventory
- **Price negotiations**: Room for negotiation; not automatic multiple offers

### Buyer-Friendly Markets
- **Calgary**: 50–70+ days average
- **Active listings**: 5–6 months of inventory
- **Price negotiations**: Significant room to negotiate; no bidding wars typical

## Mortgage Rate Context — Q1 2026

**Bank of Canada Policy Rate:** 4.25% (as of Jan 2026)

### Fixed-Rate Mortgage Rates
- **5-year fixed (most common)**: ~5.5–5.8%
- **3-year fixed**: ~5.2–5.5%
- **7-year fixed**: ~5.8–6.1%
- **10-year fixed**: ~6.0–6.3%

### Variable-Rate Mortgage Rates
- **Prime + 0%**: ~7.0–7.2% (prime-based floating)
- **T-Bills + spread**: ~5.2–5.8% (lower, but resets quarterly)
- **Risk:** Variable rates save money if BoC cuts, but increase payments if rates rise

### Renewal Context
- Mortgages renewing in 2025–2026 typically see 1.5–2.5% increase from their original rate
- First-time buyers often lock in 5-year fixed to avoid rate shock during early ownership years
- Investors and experienced buyers more likely to use variable for lower initial payments

## Down Payment Requirements (CMHC Rules)

### CMHC Mortgage Insurance Thresholds

| Purchase Price | Minimum Down | Insurance Premium | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| Under $500K | 5% | 2.80–3.20% | CMHC required |
| $500K–$999K | 5% on first $500K, 10% on remainder | 2.80–4.00% | CMHC required |
| $1M+ | 20% minimum | None | No insurance (conventional) |

**Example:** Buying a $600K home with 10% down = $60K down, $540K mortgage. CMHC premium ~$15,120 (2.8%) added to mortgage.

### Key Rules
- CMHC insurance is mandatory for any down payment under 20%
- Premium is added to the mortgage amount and amortized
- Insurance protects the **lender**, not the buyer — if you default, you still owe the premium
- Some alternative insurers (Sagen, Canada Guaranty) exist but CMHC is most common

## Buying Power & Affordability Scenarios

### Example: First-Time Buyer, $500K Home, $250K Down (50%)
- **Mortgage needed:** $250K
- **At 5.5% fixed, 25-year amortization:** ~$1,490/month
- **Estimated GDS** (mortgage + property taxes + heat): ~32% of gross income
- **Estimated TDS** (all debt): ~40% of gross income
- **Qualifying income needed:** ~$55K gross annually

### Example: First-Time Buyer, $500K Home, $50K Down (10%)
- **Mortgage needed:** $450K
- **CMHC insurance:** ~$12,600 added to mortgage → $462,600 total
- **At 5.5% fixed, 25-year amortization:** ~$2,760/month
- **Estimated GDS / TDS:** ~38% / ~45% of gross income
- **Qualifying income needed:** ~$87K gross annually
- **CMHC insurance cost is the tradeoff for entering market sooner**

### Example: Cash Buyer, $800K Property
- **No mortgage, no CMHC, no rate risk**
- **Still pays closing costs:** ~1.5–4% of purchase price = $12K–$32K
- **Closing costs cover:** land transfer tax, legal fees, title insurance, adjustments
- **Timeline advantage:** Can close quickly without lender approval

## Provincial Considerations

### Ontario
- **Land Transfer Tax:** Yes (most significant closing cost)
- **Toronto city tax:** Additional 1.5% on properties over $55K
- **Timelines:** 30–60 days typical closing (longer than other provinces due to legal complexity)

### British Columbia
- **Property Transfer Tax:** Yes (graduated scale, up to 3% for expensive properties)
- **Speculation Tax:** 1% annually on foreign or domestic investors holding under 2 years (exceptions exist)
- **Leasehold properties:** Common in Metro Vancouver; check strata fees and lease expiry dates

### Alberta
- **No provincial land transfer tax** (major advantage)
- **Oil-sector sensitive:** Economy fluctuates with energy prices
- **Inventory:** Generally more balanced than Toronto/Vancouver

### Quebec
- **Notary system:** Notaries handle closing (different from lawyer-based provinces)
- **Welcome Tax:** 0.5–1.5% depending on city
- **Bilingual market:** Many deals conducted in French; Homie may encounter French-speaking leads

### Manitoba / Saskatchewan / New Brunswick
- **No provincial land transfer tax** (competitive advantage)
- **Lower average prices** than Ontario/BC
- **Smaller markets** with less competition

## Market Timing Signals

### Seasonal Patterns
- **Spring peak (March–May):** 40–50% of annual sales volume
- **Fall peak (September–November):** 30–40% of annual sales volume
- **Winter (December–February):** 20–30% of sales; less competition, motivated sellers
- **Summer (June–August):** Moderate activity; families prioritize schools/new year start dates

### Leading Indicators
- **Mortgage rate announcements:** Bank of Canada decisions (8x yearly) move markets
- **Housing starts:** Delayed leading indicator of future inventory
- **Days on market shrinking:** Signals market tightening and price appreciation likely
- **Inventory rising:** Signals buyer advantage and price negotiation opportunity

"Spring and fall are busy seasons. If you're flexible on timing, winter is less competitive and sellers tend to be more motivated. But the right property is the right property — when you find it, be ready to move."

**On inventory:**
"Toronto and Vancouver are tight right now — under 2 months of inventory means you need pre-approval and decisiveness. Calgary and Montreal have more breathing room for negotiation."
