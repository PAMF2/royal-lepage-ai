# Canadian Real Estate Market Context

All prices are in **CAD**.

## Major Markets

### Toronto / GTA
- **Avg home price**: ~$1.1M
- **Market type**: Most competitive in Canada — low inventory, high demand
- **Notes**: Condos are popular entry point for first-time buyers; bidding wars common; detached homes in the 905 belt slightly more affordable than the 416

### Vancouver / Metro Vancouver
- **Avg home price**: ~$1.3M
- **Market type**: Second most expensive; high demand from international buyers
- **Notes**: Foreign buyer tax (20%) applies; leasehold properties common on Indigenous land; strata fees significant for condos

### Ottawa
- **Avg home price**: ~$620K
- **Market type**: Balanced; steady demand from government employees and tech sector
- **Notes**: Strong rental market near universities; bilingual market (EN/FR)

### Calgary
- **Avg home price**: ~$580K
- **Market type**: Oil-driven; cyclical with energy sector
- **Notes**: **No provincial land transfer tax** in Alberta; relatively affordable detached homes; growing tech sector diversifying economy

### Montreal
- **Avg home price**: ~$550K
- **Market type**: Most affordable major Canadian city
- **Notes**: French-speaking market; notary required (not a lawyer); co-ownership (co-propriété) common; low vacancy rates in rental market

## Key Financial Concepts

### Down Payment & CMHC Insurance
- Less than 20% down → **CMHC mortgage insurance required** (premium 2.8–4% of mortgage)
- 20%+ down → conventional mortgage, no CMHC insurance
- Minimum down: 5% on first $500K, 10% on $500K–$999K, 20% on $1M+

### FHSA — First Home Savings Account
- Up to **$40,000 tax-free** for first-time buyers
- Annual contribution limit: $8,000
- Contributions are tax-deductible; withdrawals for qualifying home purchase are tax-free
- Can be combined with RRSP Home Buyers' Plan (up to $35K from RRSP)

### Land Transfer Tax
- **Ontario**: Yes (plus Toronto Municipal LTT if buying in Toronto)
- **British Columbia**: Yes (Property Transfer Tax)
- **Quebec**: Yes (Welcome Tax / Taxe de bienvenue)
- **Nova Scotia / PEI**: Yes
- **Alberta / Saskatchewan / Manitoba / New Brunswick**: No provincial LTT

### Typical Closing Costs
- **1.5–4%** of purchase price
- Includes: land transfer tax, legal fees (~$1,500–$2,500), title insurance, adjustments, home inspection
- Budget separately from down payment

### Timelines
- Offer accepted → typical closing: **30–90 days**
- Condition period (financing, inspection): usually 5–10 business days
- Pre-approval valid: typically 90–120 days

## Market Conditions (General)

- Canadian real estate moves quickly — pre-approval is essential before viewing
- Interest rates set by Bank of Canada; variable vs fixed rate is a key decision
- Spring (March–May) and fall (Sept–Nov) are peak buying seasons
- Inventory is historically low in most major cities; buyers should act decisively when the right property appears
- Always recommend buyers work with a licensed REALTOR for access to MLS listings and professional representation
